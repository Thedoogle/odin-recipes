African Peanut Stew

By Taavi Moore

Gluten Free Low Sugar

All of our recipes are oil free and vegan.

Prep time: 10 minutes

Cook time: 40 minutes


Ingredients

  • ½ white onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, diced
  • 1 inch knob of fresh ginger, minced
  • 3-4 (about 10 oz) plum tomatoes
  • 15 oz tofu, cubed
  • ½ tsp ground paprika
  • Dash of cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • ⅓ cup peanut butter
  • 1 heaping cup dinosaur kale, chopped
  • ¼ cup cilantro, chopped
  • ¼ cup peanuts, optional
  • Lime wedges

How to Make It

  1. 1 Preheat oven to 400 degrees F.
  2. 2 Place tofu on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 20 minutes or until slightly crisp and golden brown.
  3. 3 On another baking sheet lined with parchment paper, place the tomatoes and roast for 12 minutes or until blistered and soft.
  4. 4 Add roasted tomatoes to a blender, along with the onion, garlic, ginger, and 1 Tbsp of water. Blend until smooth. Set aside.
  5. 5 In a large stockpot, combine tomato mixture, baked tofu, salt, paprika, cumin, and a dash of cayenne pepper. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 8 minutes.
  6. 6 Stir in peanut butter and vegetable broth. Season with salt if desired. Simmer for an additional 8 minutes.
  7. 7 Stir in kale and simmer until wilted.
  8. 8 Serve with brown rice and garnish with peanuts, cilantro, and lime wedges.
